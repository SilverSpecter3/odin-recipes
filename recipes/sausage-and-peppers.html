Sausage & Mother****ing Peppers

This is a really good recipe for sausage and peppers based on one I found online a few years ago. I haven't eaten meat in a while, but you can just sub out he plant-based sausages with any kind you like. That said, I highly recommend trying the Beyond Meat sausages links. As a former heavy meat eater, I can say they taste very authentic. Probably the most authentic tasting plant-based meat alternative I have ever had.

Now to anyone wondering why would I eat a vegan sausage if I gave up meat years ago. Well, you little shit, flavor is not one of the common reasons people giveup animal products. Also, I have never seen an animal in the shape of a sausage link or a burger patty so go kick rocks if you don't like the alternatives that exist now.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon of your favorite high heat oil
  • 1 package Beyond Meat Sweet Italian or Hot Italian Sausage (not really spicy at all). Even Brat Original would work fine.
  • 1 green bell pepper, batonnet cut
  • 1 red bell pepper, batonnet cut
  • 2 sweet onions, batonnet cut
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 (14.5-ounce) can diced tomatoes or dice some fresh tomatoes yourself you lazy heifer. Roma/plum and Ugly Ripe tomatoes work really well with this. The name of the tomato is acutally called Ugly Ripe. It's what's on the inside that counts.
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes

Steps

  1. Add vegetable oil to a large pan and heat over medium-high heat. Add sausages and cook until browned on all sides. Remove sausage from pan and set aside (Note: You do not need to cook it through at this point.)
  2. Add onion and peppers to grease left in pan and cook over medium heat until softened. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  3. Slice sausage into 1 to 1 1/2-inch pieces.
  4. Return sausage to pan along with all other ingredients.
  5. Cook for 5 to 10 minutes, until sausage is cooked through and sauce is thickened.
  6. Serve on hoagie rolls, over la pasta, or on it's own with whatever sides you like.
